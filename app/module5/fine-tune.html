Fine-Tuning Large Language Models (LLMs)

What is Fine-Tuning?

Fine-tuning involves adjusting pre-trained LLMs to perform specific tasks or adapt to domain-specific data. It helps leverage the general knowledge encoded in large models for specialized applications without extensive training.

Techniques and Approaches

Common fine-tuning techniques include:

  • Task-specific dataset preparation
  • Adjusting model architecture (e.g., adding task-specific layers)
  • Hyperparameter tuning and regularization
  • Transfer learning from related tasks or domains

Applications and Best Practices

Fine-tuning LLMs is used in various applications, such as:

  • Customizing chatbots and virtual assistants
  • Enhancing content generation and summarization
  • Improving sentiment analysis and recommendation systems
  • Adapting models to new languages or domains
